Research Experience

   I have an interdisciplinary research background ranging from molecular biology to nanotechnology. I am also learning basic computational chemistry during my free time. My curiosity about new areas motivates me to do more research. Let me introduce my research experience briefly.

Structural and Molecular biology

   The common sense that structure determines all things' properties, from materials to biomacromolecule, makes me curious about the protein structure. Also, the desire to learn more biological experiments makes me choose to join Prof. Congzhao Zhou’s lab in the department of biology. There, I learned many biological techniques with the help of many seniors and did two projects. One is about LicA protein and drugs complex structure; I obtained the pure soluble protein LicA by using the E.coli expression system and purifying it. Further, I acquired the LicA protein crystal using the vapor diffusion hanging drop method and optimized the crystallization conditions. The other one is about a kind of ABC family protein, which is a membrane protein. I constructed the recombinant vector successfully by the homologous recombination method.

   I further went to Prof. Nieng Yan’s lab in Princeton in the summer of 2019 to do more systematic experiments about structural biology. I constructed two kinds of plasmids that express the same protein but with different tags and further obtained the membrane protein using the HEK-23 cell expression system. I also helped another senior with his work and got a mutated membrane protein structure in nanodisc via cryo-EM. I learned to get a mutated protein via overlap PCR and the procedure of reconstitution for the protein on Relion.

Nanotechnology and Translational Medicine

    After studying molecular biology techniques for almost a year, I learned a lot about molecular biology and wanted to do something new on the chemical aspect. So, I joined Prof. Yuanzeng Min’s lab in the department of chemistry. Min's lab is using chemical methods to solve the problem in cancer therapy. The knowledge I learned from molecular biology helped me simplify many lab protocols, such as cancer cell antigen extraction and nanoparticle synthesis. Due to my ability to learn quickly and the papers I read, I designed my first project about nanogel in cancer therapy. Now I am testing the nanogel for 4T1 breast cancer treatment in the mice model.

   During the pandemic, I read many papers at home and wrote a review with my seniors. I am also collaborating with seniors on multiple projects and further learn a lot of techniques, such as CRISPR and organic synthesis. My daily reading about the paper gives me many new ideas about this area. I plan to test some of them in the future, such as combining solid synthesis with chemotherapy and the proteoliposome applied to cancer immunotherapy.
